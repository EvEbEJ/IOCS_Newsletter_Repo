About IOCS Newsletters

IOCS newsletters are a series of weekly e-newsletters produced by the International Organization of Creation Science. They feature God's amazing animals and the creation-evolution controversy.

IOCS newsletters always include these columns:

  • A front picture introduces each newsletter. This picture displays the intricate design of God. If the photo is not working for you, you can always check out the newsletter here at our website. A second option is to view it on a social media platform. To view the photo on Instagram, simply click on it. To view it on Pinterest, click the link in the description.

  • Every newsletter includes a fun fact about science, nature, and anything related to creation. Each fact is submitted by readers and reviewed by us. With stunning graphics, this section is a sure eyecatcher!

  • The first article of each newsletter is titled Evolution Questioned. It explains a common area in the creation-evolution controversy from a biblical perspective, often highlighting what God can show us through his work.

  • Another article is called Dinosaur the of Week (formerly known as Dinosaurs of Eden). This column explains facts about dinosaurs, how they go along with the Bible, and much more! It is an eye-opener, and usually discusses facts crucial to a creationist's faith.

  • Exploring the Fossils is a monthly column displaying how fossils refute the very idea of evolutionism. It often links the fossil with God's word, and gives incredibly detailed and picturesque descriptions.

  • Civil Discourse is also monthly. This column focuses on the construction of a good argument, especially in terms of the creation-evolution debate.

  • The final article is known as Creature of the Week. Every week, one creature is discussed and explored. Ultimately, every animal points back to the Creator, and this article boldens it in full.

  • The articles are followed up by a fun and often entertaining comic strip aptly named "Creature Comics." Every week, these catchy comics illustrate a key principle in the creation-evolution controversy.

  • Currently, all newsletters conclude with a inspiring quote, which is often taken from the Bible or a famous scientist. Each quote is by no means the least of the newsletter, as in just a few sentences, it describes what others do in books.
