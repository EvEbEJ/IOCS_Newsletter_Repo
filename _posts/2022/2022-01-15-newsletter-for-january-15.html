    One of the most beautiful and fierce animals that God created is the cat.     The Survivors of the 5 Mass Extinctions

    5 is a big number. Can two creatures survive Evolution and old mother nature's 5 mass extinctions? Find out who they are and if they can HERE!

    Evolution readily teaches 5 mass extinctions. Today we are going to talk about two creatures, dating back some 480 million years, that have survived all 5 mass extinctions: the sea lily (Crinoidea) and the sea star (Asteroidea). This doesn’t appear to be plausible, does it? That’s right, it doesn't. We shall see why.

    The oldest mass extinction, the Ordovician-Silurian extinction, took place around 445 million years ago to 415 million years ago. During this period, 85% of all life supposedly died on earth. This implies that only 15% of all life during the first mass extinction survived. The Ordovician-Silurian extinction is said to be the second greatest mass extinction on earth. This extinction is suggested to be caused by toxic metals including iron, arsenic, and lead released on the earth, posing a threat to all marine life. This would also put sea stars and sea lilies at risk. Yet even according to National Geographic, evidence is lacking for the Ordovician-Silurian extinction.

    Evolution claims that the Devonian extinction took place around 383 million years ago and lasted 20 million years. During this period, it is said that 75% of all life on earth, meaning only 25% of life survived. Somehow, both of the echinoderms, sea stars and sea lilies, are still hanging in there. The Devonian extinction is said to have occurred as a result of volcanic activities. Greenhouse gases such as sulfur dioxide could have contributed, as well as asteroid compacts. One compact, the Siljan crater in Sweden, is 32 miles wide. Occurring 377 million years ago, this crash into the earth is also credited to the Devonian extinction. Still, these dates and the blame of the Devonian extinction are not credible, and could be, and probably are false labels.

    What’s the Deal With Carbon Dating?

    Is the 'the fundamental premise of carbon-14 dating' flawed? Find out HERE!


    If you have been involved in Evolution discourse at any point in time, you have probably heard the phrase ‘Carbon Dating’ thrown around a couple times. This article will tackle what Carbon Dating is, its reliability, and why it is so important to evolution discussions.

    Carbon dating is a method scientists use to predict the age of a dead organism. Here is how it works: When an organism is alive, it consumes carbon. Most of this carbon is carbon-12, which has a stable amount of neutrons. However, some carbon is an unstable isotope of carbon made from nitrogen-14 in the upper atmosphere. This isotope is called Carbon-14. When an organism dies, it stops consuming carbon, and carbon-14 decays back into nitrogen-14 by emitting beta particles. Scientists measure the number of beta particles emitted and calculate, using the assumed rate of carbon-14’s decay, the age of the organisms.

    Mission


    from the IOCS Directors

    It is always important to know what you are doing. It is also important to let others know what you are doing. IOCS has been doing a lot, for example, in our newsletter we feature photos, articles, and more. But is that the IOCS' goal? To take photos or to defeat evolution?

    The answer to those questions is a definite no. We are not here to show what we can do, what we can accomplish. For if we were, we could not and would not survive very long. And even if we did, our photos and articles will have no power. They would be empty.

    But our primary focus is to glorify God in all that we do. For we can do anything with God’s strength (Philippians 4:13). God is the one on whom we are looking to all times.

    This is a reminder and reaffirmation both to IOCS members and to our readers. We are not here to elevate ourselves or our organization, but to reach the unreachable (Matthew 28:19-20), and to do the impossible (Luke 1:37) to God’s glory.
