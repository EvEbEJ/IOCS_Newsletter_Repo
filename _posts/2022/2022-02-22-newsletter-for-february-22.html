    The Pepper Mild Mottle Virus

    What can be found in both humans and peppers? Do you even like peppers? Is this thing dangerous? Read more HERE!

    The Pepper Mild Mottle Virus (PMMoV) is well documented in plants, but some may not be aware that it can be found in humans. PMMoV, named so for the spots (mottling) it produces on a plantï¿½s leaves, is found in plants of the Capsicum (Pepper) family, bell and chili especially. It can also be found in Tomatoes and Eggplants. The disease is usually mild, but severity can be increased if the plant is infected young. Any reader who is a pepper farmer knows that the virus, as well as other Tobamoviruses, can be spread through human contact to healthy plants - in other words, if a farmer touches an infected plant and then a healthy plant, the healthy plant is now disease. Because PMMoV also produces necrosis and blistering on the plants, pepper-growers often use chemicals to defeat the virus.

    Mission


    from the IOCS Directors

    It is always important to know what you are doing. It is also important to let others know what you are doing. IOCS has been doing a lot, for example, in our newsletter we feature photos, articles, and more. But is that the IOCS' goal? To take photos or to defeat evolution?

    The answer to those questions is a definite no. We are not here to show what we can do, what we can accomplish. For if we were, we could not and would not survive very long. And even if we did, our photos and articles will have no power. They would be empty.

    But our primary focus is to glorify God in all that we do. For we can do anything with Godï¿½s strength (Philippians 4:13). God is the one on whom we are looking to all times.

    This is a reminder and reaffirmation both to IOCS members and to our readers. We are not here to elevate ourselves or our organization, but to reach the unreachable (Matthew 28:19-20), and to do the impossible (Luke 1:37) to Godï¿½s glory.
