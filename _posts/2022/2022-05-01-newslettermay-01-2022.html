    Surtsey - Perfect Model

    A catostrophic Flood would surely require at least a hundred thousand years for the earth to recover from, right? Actually, no. Evidence lies no more than 20 miles off the coast of Iceland. Read about it NOW!

    In 1963, there was a volcano, 20 miles off the coast of Iceland and 130 meters (426 feet) below the surface of the sea. Four years later, in 1967, this volcano had transformed into an island, called Surtsey, with, at the time, an area of 2.6 square kilometers (1 square mile) and a peak of 170 meters (558 feet). Life quickly found its way onto Surtsey.

    Crazy Cucumbers

    Are there fruits under water? If you count a Sea Cucumber as a fruit, sure. What is a Sea Cucumber? Read MORE about this crazy creature.

    Would you believe that there is a cucumber that lives underwater? It is called the sea cucumber. The sea cucumber's name comes from its unusual features. When you first look at this fascinating creature you may find that it resembles the long, juicy, green vegetable. However, that's about all the sea cucumber has in common with the salad ingredient.

    Electric Eels

    Where does electricity come from? The sky? A power plant? It also comes at lethal amounts from an eel. Read about the Electric Eel NOW

    One of the most bizarre creatures is the Electric Eel (Electrophorus electricus). The way that it got its name is pretty obvious - it can emit electricity. Take a look at the scientific name of the electric eels, 'Electrophorus electricus." Notice that it contains many forms of 'electricity!' Something tells me that electric eels have dangerous potential.
