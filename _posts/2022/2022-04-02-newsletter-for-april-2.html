    One Flood or Many?

    One Flood or Many? Canyons, cliffs, and valleys have features that support a fast movement of water at some point of their history. Evolution and Creation agree that there were flood(s), but the dividing point is how many floods there were! Was there one flood or were there many floods? Find out HERE!

    At the Dinosaur National Monument in Utah, in the Quarry Exhibit Hall, a large slab of stone is put up for display. This stone is no ordinary rock you can find in your backyard, but it is filled with thousands of dinosaur bones. This slab was excavated from the Morrison Formation, a one-million-square-kilometer (about 400,000 square miles) area of sedimentary rock that is home to many dinosaur fossil sites and discoveries. So how did all these fossils get here?

    One idea Evolution proposes is that, about 149 million years ago, the dinosaurs in this location were overwhelmed by a series of floods — floods not as catastrophic as Noah’s Flood that were separated by long extents of time. The idea of this theory is that each time a flood swept through, they laid down another layer of dinosaur remains, which built up the massive collection of fossils.
