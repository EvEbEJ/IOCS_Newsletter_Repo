    The Moarch: King of All Butterflies

    Recently categorized as endangered, the monarch butterfly has captured the attention of the news. But something else should catch our attention: the butterfly's design - by God.

    The state insect of Alabama. Danaus plexippus. The monarch butterfly. This iconic and incredibly famous American insect is also a unique specimen in the invertebrate world. Brightly colored throughout its life, the monarch begins its 6 weeks in this world as a bright-banded caterpillar, with a vivid pattern of white, yellow, and black. After a (literally) quiet period in a chrysalis, the former caterpillar re-emerges as the king: the monarch, with bright, eye-catching orange wings. The bright colors not only give this insect a regal taste, but also serves as a warning to potential predators of this its poison.

    The Woolly Mammoth

    Mammoth. This word has come to mean big, huge, gigantic! Yes, the mammoth has captured our minds. Here is a short and sweet description of this mammoth creature.

    Woolly Mammoths (Mammuthus primigenius) are large hairy elephant-like creatures that lived hundreds of thousands of years ago. Wrong! Mammoths surely did not live this far back. How do we know this? As it turns out, many mammoths that fell into glaciers or ravines have been found by paleontologists nearly perfectly preserved! No animal could ever have stayed preserved for that long of a time.
