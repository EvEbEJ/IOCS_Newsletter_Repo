    A Plant With a Dark Side

    Some plants have unique characteristics. Some are carnivourous. Some are greedy for power. Check out a fascinating plant HERE!

    Lily pads are one of the most renowned plants in the United States. These aquatic plants are located in swamps, ponds, and lakes. The Giant Water Lily, (family Nymphaeaceae) is not your ordinary lily pad. These massive plants produce leaves with a massive diameter of 4 to 6 feet (1 to 2 meters) across. When given the chance, these plants will do everything it takes to dominate.

    When the first leaf reaches the surface of the water, its appearance is quite menacing. Sharp spikes protrude from the underside of the folded leaves. To make space for themselves, they push away competing plants by wielding these spiky, folded leaves around like a club. After the leaf blooms, the leaf’s spikes push downward, crushing, skewering, or dispatching any plant within its circumference. Soon, more leaves will appear and continue the decimation of their neighboring plants. Before long, because of the vast size of the leaves, the Giant Water Lily will block out light from the depths of the swamp or lake, eliminating any opportunity for a developing plant.

    The Rafflesia Arnoldii

    Flowers dominate the earth. They are often noted for their fragrance and color. But not all obatin this attribute. Read about a unique flower HERE!

    “Then God said, “Let the land sprout with vegetation—every sort of seed-bearing plant, and trees that grow seed-bearing fruit. These seeds will then produce the kinds of plants and trees from which they came.” And that is what happened. The land produced vegetation—all sorts of seed-bearing plants, and trees with seed-bearing fruit. Their seeds produced plants and trees of the same kind. And God saw that it was good.” Genesis 1:11-12 (NLT)

    One of these magnificent plants that the Lord our God made on the fifth day of creation was the rare, bright orange Rafflesia Arnoldii. This flower is known as the biggest flower in the world! It can grow to be 1 meter (about 3 feet) across and can weigh up to 6.8 kilograms (about 15 pounds)! This flower is the biggest of all the rafflesia, the largest of the genus, and the biggest flower on Earth. There are 28 different species of rafflesia, Malaysia and Indonesia have eight species each. Rafflesia can also be found in Thailand and the Philippines. But this rare species, the Rafflesia Arnoldii, can only be found in the rainforests of Indonesia.

    What’s the Deal With Carbon Dating?

    Is the 'the fundamental premise of carbon-14 dating' flawed? Find out HERE!


    If you have been involved in Evolution discourse at any point in time, you have probably heard the phrase ‘Carbon Dating’ thrown around a couple times. This article will tackle what Carbon Dating is, its reliability, and why it is so important to evolution discussions.

    Carbon dating is a method scientists use to predict the age of a dead organism. Here is how it works: When an organism is alive, it consumes carbon. Most of this carbon is carbon-12, which has a stable amount of neutrons. However, some carbon is an unstable isotope of carbon made from nitrogen-14 in the upper atmosphere. This isotope is called Carbon-14. When an organism dies, it stops consuming carbon, and carbon-14 decays back into nitrogen-14 by emitting beta particles. Scientists measure the number of beta particles emitted and calculate, using the assumed rate of carbon-14’s decay, the age of the organisms.
