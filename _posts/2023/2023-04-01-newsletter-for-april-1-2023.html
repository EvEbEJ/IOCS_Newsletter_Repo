At first glance, it may just look like some water in a hole in the ground, but hot springs are a lot more than just a hole. Also called a hydrothermal spring, hot springs get their heated water from the interior of the earth. Because of their extreme heat, hot spring water contains many dissolved minerals. The pH level of these springs can dip all the way down to 0.8 (which is very acidic). The vibrant displays of red and yellow that you see in hot springs comes from the billions of microorganisms that thrive in the heated water.
