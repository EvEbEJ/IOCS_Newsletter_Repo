    Orthoceras

    Underwater Wonders

    by Ebenezer Jiang

    Like many other fossils, the name “orthoceras” may sound millions of years old, but in reality, these sea creatures dwelt only 6000 years ago. Despite the name, orthoceras were just like modern squids and octopi, only that they, unfortunately, tumbled into extinction. Orthoceras were squid-like organisms that dwelt in a narrow and pointed shell, like a snail.

    In an effort to demote Creationism, Evolution states that orthoceras are over 400 million years old.1 They lived on earth for 200 million years before they became extinct.2 Evolution also “masterfully” insists that orthoceras were the ancestor of modern squids.

    But how can earth, much less a single orthocera, be 400 million years old? And why did they evolve out of their shells to become squids? After all, a shell for modern squids would be extremely helpful in defense and protecting their soft bodies, and it may also provide camouflage. Additionally, 200 million years is a very long time for any animal to live on the earth. Scientifically, all of these monstrous dates are really just assumptions and words, not scientific facts.

    In reality and contrary to what Evolution instructs, Creation teaches that orthoceras were made on the fifth day of the creation week, not the millions of years that Evolution so desperately requires. In Genesis 1:20-21, the Bible tells us this:

    “And God said, Let the waters bring forth abundantly the moving creature that hath life, and fowl that may fly above the earth in the open firmament of heaven. And God created great whales, and every living creature that moveth, which the waters brought forth abundantly, after their kind, and every winged fowl after his kind: and God saw that it was good.”

    Not only were fish, sea reptiles, and pinnipeds created, but every kind of marine creature; this would include the orthocera. It is explicit and clear; orthoceras are not products of Evolution and a big bang that happened out of total nothingness. Not at all! Rather, orthoceras were fashioned by the world’s Intelligent Designer, showing the power of our Creator God, the great majesty of his work, and the perfectness of his Word.

    Evolutionists might say in defense, “Well, a fossil is required to be over 10,000 years old, so in order for orthoceras to be a real fossil, it must be 10,000 years or more.”

    Really? Where is the evidence for this corrupted assumption?

    No official record (other than evolutionary texts and evolution-biased study books) makes this statement!

    The English Oxford Dictionary simply defines fossils as “the parts of a dead animal or a plant that have become hard and turned into rock.” This doesn’t mean: “the parts of a dead animal or a plant that have become hard and turned into a rock that is at least ten thousand years old.”

    As long as something is the remains of an organism, in the form of rock or another substance, it is a fossil. There is no scientific evidence that requires a fossil to be over 10,000 years old. All fossils, from plants to animals, including orthoceras, do not have to be (and likely are not) 10,000 years old.

    Tyrannosaurus

    by Christian Conti

    What’s the first creature that comes to mind when you think of the word “dinosaur”? Surely it must be the tyrant king of lizards Tyrannosaurus (tyrant lizard), the most famous species of which being T. rex (king). Being one of the largest theropods, this giant towered over even the largest of animals living today. From head to tail it grew to a staggering 40 feet (12.192 meters) and weighed in at about 9 tons (7257.48 kilograms).

    Although it is uncertain whether Tyrannosaurus was a hunter or a scavenger, we are sure that it was a carnivore since we have found crushed up bones of other dinosaurs in its coprolites (fossilized dung). (Check out our coprolite article here!) But just like every other carnivore, it was once an herbivore before the Fall.

    Tyrannosaurus’s habitat ranged mainly all around North America. Tyrannosaurus is one of the best-represented dinosaurs to date, as paleontologists have uncovered an unusually large amount of fossils from it. In fact, preserved tissue has even been found, indicating this creature is not millions of years old but actually lived alongside humans.

    The Australian Magpie

    by Ebenezer Jiang

    Above: A magpie glares its brown eyes into the camera. Photo by Earnest Law.

    Pies. How many kinds of pies are there? Cherry, apple, berry, and more! But wait! One of the most interesting pies is the Magpie. The Australian Magpie (Gymnorhina tibicen)1 is, in reality, not a pie, otherwise, it would not be in this article. These birds live everywhere in Australia, only avoiding the dense forests and drier deserts.

    There are many things about this bird that make it a unique creature. For one thing, these birds have a unique name, the Flute Bird, which corresponds to their flute-like call. Interestingly, magpies often play their melodies in groups. But that’s not all. The Australian Magpie can copy the songs of 35 different birds, other animal calls, and even human speech! The Flute Bird, or Australian Magpie, whatever you want to call it, is indeed a unique gift from God, and something to watch in awe.2

    In Psalm 86:12, it says, “I will praise thee, O Lord my God, with all my heart: and I will glorify thy name for evermore.” When we consider these words, we can echo these words, in our hearts, in our speech, and in our actions.
