    The Coelacanth

    by Ebenezer Jiang

    Little is known concerning the deep sea and its creatures. Many creatures that lurk in the depths of the ocean are exotic, bizarre, and extraordinary. One of the most amazing deep-sea-dwellers is the coelacanth. The Coelacanth1,2,3,4 (order Coelacanthiformes) is a fish of an extremely compelling background. Not only is it exotic in looks and features, but it is also a battleground between two worldviews — Creation and Evolution.

    According to Evolution, the coelacanth dwelt on the earth for over 300 million years. Because its fossils ended at what would be considered the Cretaceous period, it is assumed that coelacanths fell into extinction about 70 million years ago. The coelacanth has fins which Evolutionists aptly regarded as a transition between fins and feet. So, the coelacanth is backing up Evolution, right?

    No. It actually isn’t. Ever since 1938, many coelacanths have been fished out of the oceans. This fact conspicuously and efficiently destroys the idea that coelacanths fell into extinction millions of years ago. Additional research was done on the coelacanth; the results were astonishing. Compared to their fossils, living coelacanths are virtually unchanged! This is a nuisance to Evolution in many ways. First of all, any species living for hundreds of millions of years should have evolved! Then there’s the fact that since coelacanths are unchanged, they probably aren’t the much-needed links between land and aquatic animals, leaving Evolution on the forever-lasting wild-goose-chase for missing links. Also, if Evolution’s presupposition of burial and gradual processes was correct, we shouldn’t see any fish fossils! Another question is since the coelacanth is still alive and did not fall into extinction, why did its fossils “end” in the fossil record at the Cretaceous period? It’s all very simple. There was simply no Cretaceous period (and any other period found in Evolution’s sound-like-science library for that matter5), no millions of years, no missing links, and evidently no Evolution.

    Evolution is easily disregarded when considering the coelacanth. The coelacanth doesn’t at all back up the Evolutionary hypothesis, but rather, it points to Creation. Genesis 1 clearly and explicitly explains the creation of the universe, which includes the coelacanth. Fossils were most likely created in an event, which took place in Genesis 6 through 8. Through rapid burial and the right conditions provided by the Flood, the coelacanth has become not only an awe-striking fossil but an ever-present reminder of how God acts towards sinfulness. Everything about the coelacanth is fascinating, compelling, and revealing the work of an Intelligent, Almighty Designer.

    Echo Chambers and Strengthening Your Argument

    by Ellie Strano

    An Echo Chamber is defined by Oxford Dictionary as “an environment in which a person encounters only beliefs or opinions that coincide with their own so that their existing views are reinforced and alternative ideas are not considered.” In other words, when someone surrounds themselves with ideas that only reflect their personal, already formed belief, they are living in an Echo Chamber. Think of a person shouting their idea into a cave, only to have it echo back without challenging or changing it. An Echo Chamber is a psychological bias that prevents one from thinking critically.

    Let’s look at another example of a psychological bias: a Confirmation bias. A confirmation bias happens when you seek out information that agrees with a belief you have, instead of looking at all objective information on the subject. As an example, let’s say Tony believes in astrology.

    Tony’s zodiac tells him he will find five dollars on the ground that day. Later that morning, he finds money on the ground. Tony excitedly rants to everyone he knows that his experience is irrefutable evidence of astrology. He doesn’t remember the dozens of times when he found money but his zodiac didn’t say he would, or when he didn’t find money even though the zodiac reassured him he would. Tony already believed in astrology before he found money on the ground, so he seeks out examples that support his belief, forgetting or ignoring contrary facts.

    It’s doubtful that any reader of this newsletter has such an extreme confirmation bias like Tony’s. However, no one is immune to these psychological traps like echo chambers and biases.

    This newsletter column is about defending your argument. It will help you recognize flaws in an evolutionist’s arguments, and how to carefully construct a counter-argument. While you learn, it’s important to remember that sometimes the answer lies somewhere in the middle. Not everything is black and white, or left and right. Excluding some ideas because you assume you already know the argument instead of taking the time to understand it is tribal thinking, another psychological bias that will keep you from truly understanding God’s creation.

    So how does one avoid an Echo Chamber or another psychological bias, especially in an age when everyone is only a few clicks away from hearing whatever opinion that will confirm their bias? The answer is: by talking to people and engaging with ideas that differ from yours. Keeping your sources diverse, from all different backgrounds and schools of thought, maintains a healthy, well-tested argument, one that can stand its ground against any refutation. It is important to understand the opposing viewpoint, not only because you will avoid an echo chamber, but also because when you fully understand a worldview, you will understand how it is flawed.

    Mimicry

    by Evan Jiang

    It’s a bird! It’s a plane! Wait a minute… Mimicry, the resemblance of something else, is a characteristic displayed in a variety of creatures, by both the hunter and the hunted. So what’s the big deal with mimicry?

    There are many kinds of mimicry. We will cover two today. One type of mimicry that harmless prey often use is the imitation of something harmful. For example, some hoverflies have bright yellow warning colorations that are almost indistinguishable from stinging bees. Another kind of mimicry is one used by the predator. This kind of mimicry involves deceiving the prey by pretending to be one of them. One such example is a certain kind of ant-hunting spider, which looks and smells like its prey, a weaver ant.

    Mimicry is an example of the result of mutations and genetic change. Then doesn’t this mean that the theory of evolution is right? No. The kinds as presented in the Bible probably refer best to scientific families. Adaption within these families is just the loss of some of the original genetic diversity. God’s creation of mimicry clearly illustrates his care for all his creatures. How much more does he care for every one of us!
