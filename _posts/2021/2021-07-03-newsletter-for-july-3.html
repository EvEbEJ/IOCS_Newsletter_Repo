    A Bad Argument

    by Evan Jiang

    Not all creationist arguments are foolproof, and today, I will give you one example that I hear a lot:

    1. If humans evolved from apes, why are there still apes today?

    It is always important to know the beliefs of the other side. This faulty statement is a result of something assumed about evolution.

    The object in assumption is whether humans evolved from apes. Evolution teaches that humans and apes evolved from an ape-like ancestor, but who was not an ape. This is actually a straw man fallacy (although it is usually unintentional)1 because it exaggerates a misinterpretation. (Looking for strong creationist arguments? Check out this page for 3 Strong Reasons Evolution is not reality.)

    So this argument is not strong but can be safely discarded.

    Bivalves

    Evidence All Along1

    by Ebenezer Jiang

    Bivalves are mollusks that have two shells that guard the owner’s soft bodies. If you’ve ever been to the beach, you might have seen, found, or caught a bivalve, such as a clam or oyster, before. Throughout the world, these shellfish are very common and well known. So, it’s not surprising that there are over 15,000 living species of clams, and 500 live in freshwater.2 But clams are not only found as shellfish in sand. Not at all! They are also found as fossils in rock! Many specimens of clam fossils have been gathered throughout the world. Say, how did these fossils form? Let’s find out.

    Many people think that evolution is science, and believe in it. Really? Trying to exclude God from every part of life, Evolutionists put clam fossils under the category of millions and even billions of years. And, like for all fossils, slow and constant burial does the trick.

    Really? Is that scientific? No. Clams are bivalves, meaning that they have two shells, which are connected by a very strong muscle. When danger is sensed or if they feel threatened, clams will tightly close their shells together. After death, this muscle relaxes, and the two halves of the shell open up; this is why at the beach, it is easier to find one half of a clam than to find two complete clam shells that stayed together. Annoyingly, this factor acts as a thorn in the foot of the theory of Evolution, because if a clam lays in one place for thousands or maybe millions of years, waiting to get buried, it will undoubtedly be dead. But if this is the case, why are there clam fossils with tightly closed shells? This could only mean a quick and catastrophic burial. (Sounds like a flood?)

    It is evident and explicit. Because Evolution removes God, the evolutionary theory of millions of years proves to be no more than a fable.

    Creation gives a broad, straight, and errorless response. Like all marine creatures, clams were fashioned by God on the third day (a literal 24-hour day) of creation. Contrary to Evolution, Creationists believe that the Flood, sent by God, buried all of life on earth, except for that on the ark. This would have rapidly and catastrophically buried clams globally. This would also explain their tense, closed position. Doubtless, they were frightened by the great, pounding waves that the flood displayed. Also, the clams would have been buried alive and would have had no chance of escaping the tons of sediment poured on them.

    Even clams can show us God’s intricate design and splendor and the flawlessness of his word. Indeed, Agur, that is, the writer of Proverbs 30, was right when he said, “Every word of God is pure: he is a shield unto them that put their trust in him.”3 We can put our trust in God not only because of all the evidence in fossils and other forms that he has shown to us, but also for his errorless, trustworthy word, which he has given to us. To him be glory and honor forever and ever.

    Opabinia

    by Christian Conti

    There once was a bizarre creature that swam in the vast oceans of the Earth. It happened to be one of the strangest animals of God’s creation. Its name is Opabinia (named after Opabin pass). It has many features that make it stand out from most other arthropods. One of its most striking features is its long proboscis with a claw at its tip that was used for gripping prey. It was also useful for bringing food directly to its mouth, similar to that of the modern-day elephant. Another unusual trait was that it possessed five eyes planted on stalks.

    Most fossils of this creature have been found in the Burgess Shale fossil site, along with many other similar creatures. It was first discovered by paleontologist Charles Walcott, who found several intact fossils.

    The Garter Snake

    by Ebenezer Jiang

    Above: A garter snake in a forest. Photo by Ebenezer Jiang.

    You are hiking with your family in a forest with a dry forest bed. As you trek along, you notice an object. But even before you can stoop in to look, screams and yelps already tell you what it is, the Garter Snake. The Garter Snake (Thamnophis sirtalis)1 dwells in most of North America. These reptiles are also called Garden Snakes because they are often discovered by houses and garden beds. Garter Snakes are distinguished by their black lines on a yellow background. The yellow hue can vary from a bright yellow to a dull yellow.2

    These snakes vary in viciousness, some hesitate before striking while others (usually larger ones) give it no thought. If you ever come across these reptiles it is recommended that you watch from a safe distance. Always remember that garters would rather slither than strike.

    So, what do these snakes have to offer when they are threatened? ‘Well,’ You might say, ‘They bite!’ Yes, they do bite. But sometimes, biting is not enough, especially since it’s not venomous. Some smaller snakes can’t even pierce human skin! When this is the case, snakes have another amazing way of defense. Musking. ‘Um,’ You say, ‘Do you mean masking?’ No. Musking is when a snake secretes a foul-smelling substance to stink off its enemies. Because of its smaller size, the Garter Snake could musk more than the average snake. Often, the smell of the musk of a snake comes from the animal’s diet, and because of this, Garter Snake’s musk often smells like a dead rat, its diet. The musk is also difficult to wash away because its molecules hold tightly to whatever it lands on. In addition, this concoction tastes as bad as it smells, and so, it makes a great weapon of defense.3

    Who but God can create such a superpower? Stinky, smelly, and repulsive, but protective, defensive, and effective. It all simply points to the sureness of the ever-present Intelligent Designer, who is not only watching over his snakes but over you.
