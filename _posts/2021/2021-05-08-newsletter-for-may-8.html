    The Right Grasshopper

    by Evan Jiang

    Does natural selection actually exist, or is it just another evolutionary mechanism? Read on to find out.

    A common misconception about natural selection is that it is an extremely complex process. Although there are a few twists here and there, the core concept is simple.

    Natural selection is just that – the selection of stronger organisms in nature. This is the theme. Stronger organisms have a higher chance of survival, and therefore, will be able to have their genes passed onto the next generation. There are many real-life examples.

    Here’s one: Pink grasshoppers exist, but they are rare. They are at a disadvantage to green grasshoppers that can camouflage. So birds can see the brightly-colored pinks more easily. Being spotted is usually not good in an insect’s world, because you can get eaten. Fewer pinks getting eaten means fewer eggs. Fewer eggs mean fewer grasshoppers.

    So that’s natural selection. Green grasshoppers outmuscle pink grasshoppers because they are better equipped. Since I gave a real example, the answer to the question way at the beginning should be obvious. No. Natural selection is not just evolutionary thinking. It is a genuine, observable process and not only that. It is a perfectly legitimate way to credit our Maker with his ingenuity and intelligence.

    Coprolite

    Fetid and Beyond

    by Ebenezer Jiang

    Coprolite. A cool word, isn’t it? Although the origin of this type of fossil, coprolite, is rather fetid (literally) and often avoided, coprolite shines a lot of light on the error of evolution. Animal feces (unfossilized coprolite) dissolves and decays rather quickly, so it is shocking and surprising to find these sorts of fossils. If, as Evolution says, coprolites were formed while being slowly buried, not-yet-fossilized-coprolite wouldn’t stand a chance! They would surely dissolve by weathering, bacteria, and suffer the danger of being crushed. And this is all the more so because we have found coprolites that came from marine creatures! (We all know that water dissolves not-yet-fossilized-coprolite faster than on land.) Another problem is the "fresh appearance" of lots of coprolite. In order for the coprolite to stay in good condition, it must stay fresh and moist. But if a piece of you-know-what laid for a long time, it would undoubtedly be dried, and so, cracked and powdery. And even if the coprolite did stay fresh and wet, the feces would be no longer fresh, but muck.

    Many paleontologists and scientists fumble around, trying to explain the formation of coprolite. Evolutionists try saying that coprolites are rare; this would give some backing to gradual burial. Not going to work...coprolites are actually abundant, especially the ones which originated in water! Not to mention that this doesn’t explain how coprolites formed without a form of interruption or disturbance. Additionally, coprolites, dated back to the 9th century, have been located. This suggests that fossils form quickly. In this example, only a thousand or so years ago. So, no need to wait for the millions of years that Evolution insists on. The most adequate idea of the formation of coprolites is explained in the Bible.

    In Genesis 6-8, God tells us about the Flood. In Genesis 6:17, God says:

    “And, behold, I, even I, do bring a flood of waters upon the earth, to destroy all flesh, wherein is the breath of life, from under heaven; and every thing that is in the earth shall die.”

    Now, for Noah’s flood to kill all of humanity and life on the entire planet, the flood would have been highly catastrophic and the erosion rates would have been massive, which would result in very rapid burial. In Genesis 8:2, God says:

    “The fountains also of the deep and the windows of heaven were stopped, and the rain from heaven was restrained;”

    The “fountains of the deep” would mean that the Flood was so highly catastrophic, it would have been very different from the tropical storms we have today. The earth would have literally split open and water would have burst out. And in saying “the windows of heaven,” the rain would be basically bombing the earth. Everything would have been buried, including animal wastes, and because of the high catastrophism and erosion, no bacteria would have been able to decay the wastes. The result would be fossilized dung.

    In a last effort, one argument is: “Doesn’t water turn not-yet-fossilized-coprolite into muck? Coprolites look exactly like what their original shape was; so if the Flood, if it existed washed onto coprolite, wouldn’t they dissolve?” Yes, water does dissolve unfossilized-coprolite, and if a normal flash flood (like Evolution might suggest) washed onto coprolite, it would dissolve without a doubt. But the Flood was unlike any of the floods we have ever seen. It would have carried tons of sediment as it engulfed the earth, because of this, the coprolites would have been buried in an instant.

    This explicitly explains that all things in this universe, from the greatest beasts, even to things we wrinkle our noses at can point to an Intelligent Designer, our Creator God. Like all the other fossils, coprolite comfirms that we should do just as Psalm 9:1 instructs: "I will praise thee, O Lord, with my whole heart; I will shew forth all thy marvellous works."

    It Couldn’t Last That Long

    by Christian Conti

    The column, "Dinosaurs of Eden," is a bi-weekly article. This week's article:

    What happens to a chicken bone that gets left outside in the yard? Most of the time a dog or cat or other animal eats it or buries it in the ground. But what would happen if it were buried very quickly? It might turn into a chicken bone fossil. When it is fossilized, it will be completely replaced by minerals, and become rock solid. So, if you dug the chicken bone up, it would be a very hard, rocky replica.

    This process is common with dinosaur bones. Most scientists who believe in evolution will tell you that fossils from dinosaurs are over 60 million years old. But that simply cannot be true. Fairly recently, something happened that disproves the idea that dinosaur fossils are millions of years old. Scientists uncovered a fossil from a Tyrannosaurus Rex. But when they broke the fossil open, it still had soft tissues. They had not been replaced by rocky minerals. It is obviously not possible for soft tissue to last millions of years even if it were buried underground. It would have decayed or had been fossilized.

    The excuse scientists come up for this is that it “somehow” lasted 65 million years. But that is an outright farce. This helps us see that dinosaurs are not millions, but thousands of years old, and were created by a single God.

    The Bull Ant

    by Ebenezer Jiang

    Above: The dreaded Bull ant faces the camera with jaws wide open. Photo by Earnest Law.

    Where is the last place you would want to be? On a desert island? Lost in the Mammoth Caves? For some, it would be in the jaws of a Bull Ant. Bull Ants (Myrmecia gulosa) 1 or Bulldog Ants live in Australia. These insects are 4 centimeters long, which makes them one of the largest ants on planet Earth. Bull ants eat plant juices, nectar, and all sorts of insects and bugs. 2,3

    WARNING: Bull Ants are venomous. Bull Ants will not hesitate to deliver a painful sting. If one is stung, place an ice pack on the wound. If there are allergic reactions, contact a doctor immediately.

    The Bull Ant is renowned for its bite and sting since it is regarded as the world’s most venomous ant.4 These insects would aggressively attack anything that poses a threat.5 These creepy critters have notable, eye-catching jaws. Saw-like teeth align the jaws giving the Bull Ant a very vicious and hostile look. But that’s not all. The abdomen holds the real weapon of destruction, the stinger. The venom in the Bull ants’ stings is six times more than the amount of venom in a honey bee. The pain of a sting could last for days! But that’s not all. Some bull ants could jump 2 inches into the air to latch onto their victims! And it may be tough getting away from these insects because, unlike most ants, Bull Ants could see for up to a meter. One thing is for certain: Steer clear of the Bull Ant!

    Evidently, the Bull Ant did not evolve hundreds of millions of years ago by random chance, rather, it is clear that there was one who designed their formation, an Intelligent Designer. Even the Bull Ant magnifies Deuteronomy 3:24 (KJV): “O Lord GOD, thou hast begun to shew thy servant thy greatness, and thy mighty hand: for what God is there in heaven or in earth, that can do according to thy works, and according to thy might?” Indeed, who, but God, can do what God has done in creating what has to be the Creature of the Week, the Bull Ant?
