    Is Evolution Really Like Gravity?

    by Evan Jiang

    According to most evolutionists, the theory of evolution is well-proven —— some dare even to compare it with gravity! After all, both are theories, right? Actually, the term “theory” is pretty vague. A theory can range anywhere from a far-fetched idea (e.g. the Flat Earth theory) to the strongest fact (e.g. gravity).

    Also, gravity is experienced by everybody within the gravitational force. It’s observable. That’s why we don’t float away from the earth, and why we observe apples falling to the ground. Scientific fact results solely from observable objects and occurrences. How about evolution? Remember that the theory of evolution involves only macroevolution. We see no examples of that today (despite the classic evolutionary excuse, “It’s too slow to be noticeable”). So macroevolution is an unproven, unobservable theory that is nowhere near the credibility of the theory of gravity.

    Bivalves

    Evidence All Along1

    by Ebenezer Jiang

    Bivalves are mollusks that have two shells that guard the owner’s soft bodies. If you’ve ever been to the beach, you might have seen, found, or caught a bivalve, such as a clam or oyster, before. Throughout the world, these shellfish are very common and well known. So, it’s not surprising that there are over 15,000 living species of clams, and 500 live in freshwater.2 But clams are not only found as shellfish in sand. Not at all! They are also found as fossils in rock! Many specimens of clam fossils have been gathered throughout the world. Say, how did these fossils form? Let’s find out.

    Many people think that evolution is science, and believe in it. Really? Trying to exclude God from every part of life, Evolutionists put clam fossils under the category of millions and even billions of years. And, like for all fossils, slow and constant burial does the trick.

    Really? Is that scientific? No. Clams are bivalves, meaning that they have two shells, which are connected by a very strong muscle. When danger is sensed or if they feel threatened, clams will tightly close their shells together. After death, this muscle relaxes, and the two halves of the shell open up; this is why at the beach, it is easier to find one half of a clam than to find two complete clam shells that stayed together. Annoyingly, this factor acts as a thorn in the foot of the theory of Evolution, because if a clam lays in one place for thousands or maybe millions of years, waiting to get buried, it will undoubtedly be dead. But if this is the case, why are there clam fossils with tightly closed shells? This could only mean a quick and catastrophic burial. (Sounds like a flood?)

    It is evident and explicit. Because Evolution removes God, the evolutionary theory of millions of years proves to be no more than a fable.

    Creation gives a broad, straight, and errorless response. Like all marine creatures, clams were fashioned by God on the third day (a literal 24-hour day) of creation. Contrary to Evolution, Creationists believe that the Flood, sent by God, buried all of life on earth, except for that on the ark. This would have rapidly and catastrophically buried clams globally. This would also explain their tense, closed position. Doubtless, they were frightened by the great, pounding waves that the flood displayed. Also, the clams would have been buried alive and would have had no chance of escaping the tons of sediment poured on them.

    Even clams can show us God’s intricate design and splendor and the flawlessness of his word. Indeed, Agur, that is, the writer of Proverbs 30, was right when he said, “Every word of God is pure: he is a shield unto them that put their trust in him.”3 We can put our trust in God not only because of all the evidence in fossils and other forms that he has shown to us, but also for his errorless, trustworthy word, which he has given to us. To him be glory and honor forever and ever.

    The column, "Dinosaur of the Week," is a weekly article. This week's article:

    Euoplocephalus

    by Christian Conti

    Above: An artistic rendering of a Euoplocephalus.

    One of the most eye-catching and best-protected dinosaurs, Euoplocephalus (well-protected head), is a marvel of God’s creation. It is covered in thick, bony plates from head to tail, which act as natural armor that can break the teeth of would-be predators. Even its eyelids have armor! It is not a surprise that it is called a “living tank.” All over this creature are large spikes, which deter attackers. To top it all off, it also has a large bony club that it swings from side to side with its muscular tail that can shatter the legs of predators, like its rival Albertosaurus, a genus of Tyrannosaur.

    Euoplocephalus is a fairly common fossil among others. It has been found all over North America, but especially in Alberta, Canada, where its first fossil was found. Lawrence Lambe was the first to find a fossil in 1897.

    Euoplocephalus was strictly an herbivore. It feasted on low-lying vegetation that it could reach. It had a bulky body, and short legs and neck, which made it difficult to reach higher vegetation. Euoplocephalus was 23 feet (7.0104 meters) long and about the size of a rhinoceros, but probably much heavier thanks to the armor. With all this extra weight it needed strong legs, but still probably could not run very fast. If threatened, Euoplocephalus did not run but most likely stood its ground.

    The Robber Fly

    by Ebenezer Jiang

    Above: A Robber Fly awaits in ambush on a pine needle. Photo by Evan Jiang.

    There are many strange names for flies. There’s the Horse Fly, the Soldier Fly, the Bee Fly, the House Fly, and much more. But perhaps the strangest of all such names is the Robber Fly (family Asilidae).1 Robber Flies’ name certainly gives the insect a bad reputation. These flies are about 1.25 inches long (4 cm). There are an estimated 5,500 species that dwell on our planet, whose range dot all around the globe.

    Although rare, Robber Flies could bite if irritated or threatened. Remember to stay clear of those razor-sharp proboscises.2

    Robber Flies are hunters. They use their mouthparts to drink the juices of their victims. But first, they have to locate and catch their prey. This is no problem for the Robber Fly. These Intelligently-designed flies have keen eyes for focusing on their prey. Well, once located, they need to snag the prey. Piece of cake. These insects can use their agility in the air or stalk their prey on land. They often chase down their menu, which includes the feared dragonfly!

    Though the name sounds negative, Robber Flies certainly obtain a wonderful superpower that none can describe. It is obvious to all that an Almighty Maker designed this intricate insect.
