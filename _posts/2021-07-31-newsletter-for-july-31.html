    The Mission of IOCS

    from the IOCS Directors

    It is always important to know what you are doing. It is also important to let others know what you are doing. IOCS has been doing a lot, for example, in our newsletter we feature photos, articles, and more. But is that the IOCS' goal? To take photos or to defeat evolution?

    The answer to those questions is a definite no. We are not here to show what we can do, what we can accomplish. For if we were, we could not and would not survive very long. And even if we did, our photos and articles will have no power. They would be empty.

    But our primary focus is to glorify God in all that we do. For we can do anything with God’s strength (Philippians 4:13). God is the one on whom we are looking to all times.

    This is a reminder and reaffirmation both to IOCS members and to our readers. We are not here to elevate ourselves or our organization, but to reach the unreachable (Matthew 28:19-20), and to do the impossible (Luke 1:37) to God’s glory.

    De-evolution

    by Evan Jiang

    According to the evolutionary theory, animals grow more complex and gain more genes. In addition, the world is getting better. Single-cell animals evolve into multicellular organisms. Bacteria evolve into people. But is this what we see?

    As always, each species produces only after its own kind, but there’s more! All creatures are losing genetic diversity. For example, an ostrich cannot fly because it has lost the gene to produce strong wings. This is not evolution because:

    1. The ostrich is still a bird. It has not evolved into a mammal, fish, etc.
    2. The ostrich has lost a function. It can no longer fly. The losing of a function is what creationists would call ‘de-evolution.’

    De-evolution is the opposite of evolution, yet we see it happening all around us. So can we actually observe evolution?

    Bivalves

    Evidence All Along1

    by Ebenezer Jiang

    Bivalves are mollusks that have two shells that guard the owner’s soft bodies. If you’ve ever been to the beach, you might have seen, found, or caught a bivalve, such as a clam or oyster, before. Throughout the world, these shellfish are very common and well known. So, it’s not surprising that there are over 15,000 living species of clams, and 500 live in freshwater.2 But clams are not only found as shellfish in sand. Not at all! They are also found as fossils in rock! Many specimens of clam fossils have been gathered throughout the world. Say, how did these fossils form? Let’s find out.

    Many people think that evolution is science, and believe in it. Really? Trying to exclude God from every part of life, Evolutionists put clam fossils under the category of millions and even billions of years. And, like for all fossils, slow and constant burial does the trick.

    Really? Is that scientific? No. Clams are bivalves, meaning that they have two shells, which are connected by a very strong muscle. When danger is sensed or if they feel threatened, clams will tightly close their shells together. After death, this muscle relaxes, and the two halves of the shell open up; this is why at the beach, it is easier to find one half of a clam than to find two complete clam shells that stayed together. Annoyingly, this factor acts as a thorn in the foot of the theory of Evolution, because if a clam lays in one place for thousands or maybe millions of years, waiting to get buried, it will undoubtedly be dead. But if this is the case, why are there clam fossils with tightly closed shells? This could only mean a quick and catastrophic burial. (Sounds like a flood?)

    It is evident and explicit. Because Evolution removes God, the evolutionary theory of millions of years proves to be no more than a fable.

    Creation gives a broad, straight, and errorless response. Like all marine creatures, clams were fashioned by God on the third day (a literal 24-hour day) of creation. Contrary to Evolution, Creationists believe that the Flood, sent by God, buried all of life on earth, except for that on the ark. This would have rapidly and catastrophically buried clams globally. This would also explain their tense, closed position. Doubtless, they were frightened by the great, pounding waves that the flood displayed. Also, the clams would have been buried alive and would have had no chance of escaping the tons of sediment poured on them.

    Even clams can show us God’s intricate design and splendor and the flawlessness of his word. Indeed, Agur, that is, the writer of Proverbs 30, was right when he said, “Every word of God is pure: he is a shield unto them that put their trust in him.”3 We can put our trust in God not only because of all the evidence in fossils and other forms that he has shown to us, but also for his errorless, trustworthy word, which he has given to us. To him be glory and honor forever and ever.

    The column, "Dinosaur of the Week," is a weekly article. This week's article:

    Livyatan

    by Christian Conti

    Above: A rendering of Livyatan.

    At first glance, Livyatan may seem like just another sperm whale, but it is in fact very different. The name “Livyatan” is Hebrew for “Leviathan,” the giant sea creature described in Job. Its full name, Livyatan melvillei, is named after the famous author Herman Melville, who wrote the book, Moby Dick. It describes a monstrous white sperm whale.

    Livyatan has several features that make it stand out from sperm whales. One of these features is its gigantic teeth. These teeth are so large in fact that they are the largest in the animal kingdom, at 14 inches long! Unlike the sperm whale, Livyatan had teeth on the top jaw and the bottom jaw, indicating that the bulk of its diet was not slippery squid and fish but sea mammals, and some large fish. Livyatan also lacked a prominent spermaceti organ.

    Livyatan had an unusually large brain cavity, indicating it was very intelligent. Its physiology is actually more similar to the orca than the sperm whale. It may have also behaved similarly too, including pack hunting and social bonds. Livyatan reached sizes comparable to the sperm whale. This combination of brawn, brain, and size made it a terrifying apex predator that all creatures of the sea must have feared.

    The Bison

    by Evan Jiang

    Above: A pair of bison graze on grasses in Yellowstone. Photo by Daniel Goh.

    American Bison (Bison bison)1 are large, furry bovines that live throughout the American Midwest. They display short horns and a mess of brown fur. Although near-sighted, bison have an excellent sense of hearing and smell, and can reach speeds of up to 35 mph (55 kph)!2 American Bison migrated from Eurasia across the Bering Sea over an ice bridge that connected Russia with Alaska.

    Bison are extremely hardy creatures, eating mostly grasses, but also accepting weeds and woody plants. Their unique design allows them to digest low-quality foods more easily than other animals. Instead of just a one-chambered stomach, a bison’s stomach is divided into many chambers. Inside these chambers are microorganisms that help to break down food material into basic compounds.3 This way of digesting is especially efficient for this one-ton animal.

    Bison are truly designed by God. All of God’s creation shows his glory (Psalm 19:1).
