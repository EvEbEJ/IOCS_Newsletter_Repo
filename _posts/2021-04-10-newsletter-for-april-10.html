Micro-What?

by Evan Jiang

Microevolution this… microevolution that… but what is microevolution?

Microevolution is the variation within a kind (such as dogs, cats, etc.) Kinds differ from species in that all members of a kind may interbreed, while species are human given categories that are separated based on physical characteristics.1 An example is this: Zebras and donkeys make zonkeys. How about zebras and lizards? Zizards? I don’t think so… The difference can be seen clearly now. But to clarify it even more, take this example. Two animals that are exactly the same but live in different places are classified as two different species.

Creationists believe that microevolution formed the diverse animalkind we observe today. Two representatives of every kind (except the sacrificial birds) were brought on Noah’s ark. Let’s take the cats. The cats on the ark had a wide variety of genes, since they were closer to creation. Since they had many genes, their children would be vastly different. After the flood, their offspring spread across the globe. If two species of cats both ended up in a shady forest, but one had sandy fur, but the other had dark splotches, the sandy cat population would diminish, as it would have a harder chance at catching prey and hiding from predators. This is how each animal is perfectly suited for its own environment.

God definitely designed microevolution for his own glory. For “All the earth shall worship thee, and shall sing unto thee; they shall sing to thy name.”2

Petrified Wood

Evolution Petrified

by Ebenezer Jiang

Wood is used for many things. Most houses, fences, tables, chairs, and shelves are all made of wood. In fact, 7% of the surface of the earth is trees (that’s over 1 million square miles!)! Unsurprisingly, since the earth is covered with trees, we also find many fossils of trees, which we call “petrified wood,” which appears to be wood, but is really rock.

According to evolution, trees began to peek from the earth 400 million years ago. They claim that the petrified wood forms when trees fall and are buried with sediments, such as mud and volcanic ash, and after a few million years, the trees would become petrified wood.

This is invalid. We have observed real specimens of petrified wood that still have splinters on them! This could only form by a quick, rapid, air-tight burial. Otherwise, splinters would be weathered away by constant erosion (consider driftwood).

Then, there is also the entire bacteria and decomposition problem.

Additionally, we see petrified tree fossils that stand in many layers of rock, which evolutionists claim to have formed over millions and billions of years. Right. So, how could a tree stand up for millions of years again? The oldest tree that is still standing is younger than 10,000 years old. 10,000 years only 1% of a million years. So how can a tree stand up for a million years, much less the millions of years that it would take for a single layer of rock to be laid!

Not to mention the fact that trees that have fallen and lie around usually make an extraordinarily lovely home for bugs and worms. In petrified wood, we don’t often find bugs. This means that fresh trees were fossilized and that some trees were not lying around for millions of years.

All of this evolutionary talk is in reality human ideas and thought and not true science.

According to Creation, all trees were buried by a global, catastrophic flood. This makes sense because then layers of sediment could be laid down very rapidly. Fortunately for trees, petrified wood no longer has to stand for a million years. Also, the flood occurred about 6000 years ago, and if the flood destroyed all the trees that had begun to grow before the flood, then it would make sense if the oldest living tree is 5000 years old, which should have sprouted soon after the flood.

Even trees, simple though they seem, the things that we see every day, can display the perfectness of God, and the extreme error of evolution.

Leviathans and Behemoths

by Christian Conti

The column, "Dinosaurs of Eden," is a bi-weekly article. Last week's article:

Something to consider is chapter 40 of Job, in the Bible. God describes a creature that matches the physical attributes of some species of dinosaurs. While God is talking to Job, God asks him if he had seen the “behemoth.” God describes the animal by saying:

“Behold now behemoth, which I made with thee; he eateth grass as an ox. Lo now, his strength is in his loins, and his force is in the navel of his belly. He moveth his tail like a cedar: the sinews of his stones are wrapped together. His bones are as strong pieces of brass; his bones are like bars of iron. He is the chief of the ways of God: he that made him can make his sword to approach unto him.”1

Certainly, no animal with the likes of these walks the earth today. Many dinosaurs definitely had “tails like cedar trees,” and “bones like beams of brass.” Some people would argue that the creature described by God could be a hippopotamus or an elephant, but these creatures undoubtedly do not have “tails like cedar trees.” Hippopotamus and elephants and have tiny stubby tails. And although the elephant would seem to be “the first of the works of God,” because of its immense weight of about 14,000 pounds, it is dwarfed by the titanic sauropods, weighing in at about 150,000 pounds. The truth is, there is no animal that has ever walked the earth that is more fitting to this description than a dinosaur.

In the book of Job, we can also read of another creature that sounds very similar to a dinosaur-like, water-living reptile. God calls this creature “Leviathan.” He describes the animal to Job in chapter 41:

By his neesings [sneezings] a light doth shine, and his eyes are like the eyelids of the morning. Out of his mouth go burning lamps, and sparks of fire leap out. Out of his nostrils goeth smoke, as out of a seething pot or caldron. His breath kindleth coals, and a flame goeth out of his mouth.2

Sharp stones are under him: he spreadeth sharp pointed things upon the mire. He maketh the deep to boil like a pot: he maketh the sea like a pot of ointment. He maketh a path to shine after him; one would think the deep to be hoary. Upon earth there is not his like, who is made without fear.3

What is this incredible creature God described in his conversation with Job? It is certainly like no other creature alive today. Some people believe that this creature could be a crocodile or a whale. But whales do not have “sharp stones" on their underside, and crocodiles do not have “burning lamps" that come from their mouths. This animal was a sea monster that lived not millions of years ago, but thousands of years ago when Job lived. It was probably a mosasaur or pliosaur, gigantic, oceanic, a reptilian predator that apparently breathed fire. Many people think that this creature is imaginary, but God talks about it along with other animals like the behemoth, the wild donkey, the ox, mountain goats, horses, lions, and hawks. The leviathan and behemoth were just another part of God’s glorious creation just like the other beasts of the land, sea, and air.

Surely the mountains bring him forth food, where all the beasts of the field play. He lieth under the shady trees, in the covert of the reed, and fens.4

Canst thou draw out leviathan with an hook? or his tongue with a cord which thou lettest down?5

The Tufted Titmouse

by Ebenezer Jiang

Above: A beaver splashes through the brook. Photo by Ebenezer Jiang.

Angled tree stumps are all around the river that you are standing by. Who did it? A ripple radiates throughout the river. What was that! A head that peeked above the water soon after reveals the animal… A commonly known animal is the Beaver. Beavers (Castor canadensis) live in a variety of wetlands, mainly streams and rivers. Beavers eat leaves and twigs and other plants.1

Above: Two trees show the telltale sign of a beaver's presence. The stump is angled sharply. Both photos by Ebenezer Jiang.

Beavers evidently display the marvelous creation of God. These rodents have great architectural skills. Beavers build massive dams out of trees and twigs, which greatly surprises onlookers. With their large, plate-like tails, beavers splat mud onto their logs to help aid the success of a dam. After the dam raises the water level to a certain level, beavers could begin to construct their houses. Well, how did these creatures log wood to create their works of skill? The answer is simple, but astounding. Beavers use their teeth like a saw to cut bit by bit into a tree. With great persistence, beavers could log a tree as large as 33 inches (almost 3 feet) in diameter!2 These feats are colossal, considering the size of the rodents compared to trees. Could natural selection or evolution honestly create such an intelligent critter by chance? Never.

This all magnifies the glory of God. Psalm 24 says, “The earth is the Lord's, and the fulness thereof; the world, and they that dwell therein.” How right these words are when considering the beaver, who shows the care that God entrusts upon his creation. These rodents show that the creation was intelligently designed by an Intelligent Designer.
