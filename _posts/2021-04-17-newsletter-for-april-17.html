Irreducible Complexity

by Evan Jiang

Have you ever seen something so complex that if it were not put together by someone with the knowledge, it would not work? Have you come across anything in which the parts, which are perfectly synchronized, are missing one part, would fall apart?

This is the concept of irreducible complexity –– Complexity that cannot be taken apart. If irreducibly complex organisms or functions existed, evolution would have a huge wall to jump. Why? Evolution says that organisms were built step by step, from simple compounds to complex creatures. In other words, evolution builds step by step. Now, irreducibly complex objects do not work unless all the components are there. Suppose evolution was making a clock. Now clocks are made of gears. If even one gear was missing… the whole clock would go bonkers.

Here’s an extremely simple example. Most animals have lungs and a heart. If the heart evolved first, it would not be able to obtain any oxygen. However, if the lungs evolved first, they would die because without a heart, there is no blood circulation. With no oxygen getting to the cells, the whole organism dies.

Therefore, irreducible complexity is exceedingly difficult to overcome for evolution. Just as a light bulb without light would not exist, so also animals only partly evolved won’t exist either. They would have to be fully functional to be the fittest. Evolution does not guarantee such a luxury to links, instead, it offers death. Therefore, everything in all creation shows that there really is a God.

Petrified Wood

Evolution Petrified

by Ebenezer Jiang

Wood is used for many things. Most houses, fences, tables, chairs, and shelves are all made of wood. In fact, 7% of the surface of the earth is trees (that’s over 1 million square miles!)! Unsurprisingly, since the earth is covered with trees, we also find many fossils of trees, which we call “petrified wood,” which appears to be wood, but is really rock.

According to evolution, trees began to peek from the earth 400 million years ago. They claim that the petrified wood forms when trees fall and are buried with sediments, such as mud and volcanic ash, and after a few million years, the trees would become petrified wood.

This is invalid. We have observed real specimens of petrified wood that still have splinters on them! This could only form by a quick, rapid, air-tight burial. Otherwise, splinters would be weathered away by constant erosion (consider driftwood).

Then, there is also the entire bacteria and decomposition problem.

Additionally, we see petrified tree fossils that stand in many layers of rock, which evolutionists claim to have formed over millions and billions of years. Right. So, how could a tree stand up for millions of years again? The oldest tree that is still standing is younger than 10,000 years old. 10,000 years only 1% of a million years. So how can a tree stand up for a million years, much less the millions of years that it would take for a single layer of rock to be laid!

Not to mention the fact that trees that have fallen and lie around usually make an extraordinarily lovely home for bugs and worms. In petrified wood, we don’t often find bugs. This means that fresh trees were fossilized and that some trees were not lying around for millions of years.

All of this evolutionary talk is in reality human ideas and thought and not true science.

According to Creation, all trees were buried by a global, catastrophic flood. This makes sense because then layers of sediment could be laid down very rapidly. Fortunately for trees, petrified wood no longer has to stand for a million years. Also, the flood occurred about 6000 years ago, and if the flood destroyed all the trees that had begun to grow before the flood, then it would make sense if the oldest living tree is 5000 years old, which should have sprouted soon after the flood.

Even trees, simple though they seem, the things that we see every day, can display the perfectness of God, and the extreme error of evolution.

The Backswimmer

by Ebenezer Jiang

Above: One of the 340 species of backswimmer parks on a leaf by some stranded duckweed. The vast quantity of species of this insect shows God’s great power. Photo by Evan Jiang.

Many bugs and insects dwell in watery marshes and ponds. Some of these include water striders, water spiders, and all sorts of larvae and pupae (mosquitos, dragonflies, caddis flies, etc.). One creature, the backswimmer, is most unique. The backswimmer (Notonectidae)1 lives worldwide, like many other aquatic bugs/insects. Backswimmers eat other insects, and sometimes, small animals, like small tadpoles and fish.

Backswimmers have many forms of transportation. In synchronization with their name, these bottom dwellers swim upside down. In addition to their boat-like built bodies, by quickly paddling their long hind legs (like oars), backswimmers zip with agility through the water. Not only are these “oars” handy for helping these insects chase a fleeing snack, but also for escape. Backswimmers can fly too! If feeling threatened and seeing no escape, backswimmers could leap out of the water and quickly glide out of sight.2 How amazing is that? A boat and plane in one! It sounds Intelligently Designed.

Even the backswimmer, which is only 0.625 inches long (≈ 1.6 cm), is cared greatly and fervently by the Creator, who granted it a deft way of transportation. Once again, Matthew 10:28-30 brings us a great reminder, that God cares for all of his creatures, but more than that, He cares for us. This is something to thank God for.
