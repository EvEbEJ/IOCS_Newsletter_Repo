Common Creation-Evolution Myths

Busted

Theistic Evolution & the Bible, Part 2

by Evan Jiang

Myth: Theistic evolution is compatible with the Bible.

Theistic evolution is consistent with the Bible, right? Not so, if you look at the Bible.

Remember that theistic evolution is the belief that God created the world using macroevolution (molecules to microbes to man), an attempt to reconcile Christianity with evolutionism.

Last week, we looked at how theistic evolution does not align with Genesis. It does not stop there. The rest of the Bible also contradicts theistic evolution.

1. The rest of the Bible references creation as literal, six days.

In the Ten Commandment, the fourth commandment (the keeping of the Sabbath) references the creation week as so: “For in six days the Lord made heaven and earth, the sea, and all that in them is, and rested the seventh day…”1

Objections to this are quickly put down when it is realized that Moses authorized both the creation account and the Ten Commandments.2 Logically, he should be using the same definition of “day.”

2. Adam is presented as the first, real human.

If theistic evolution was real, Adam would be one of many of the newly evolved humans after several billion years since the “Big Bang.”

However, Adam is consistently mentioned as the first, real human. The most obvious is in the Genesis account.

“So God created man in his own image…”3

“…there was not a man to till the ground.”4

Genesis clearly picks out humans, and it clearly says that there was not a man before God created Adam. Therefore Adam is definitely the first man.

Another time Adam is mentioned is in Luke 3, where he is presented as the first human father in Jesus’ genealogy, before many, many, real, biblical people.5

Therefore, the Bible cannot be accurate if theistic evolution is true, since it refers to creation as a literal, six-day week.

Dinosaurs and Humans

by Christian Conti

As we know, in Genesis chapter 8 that the Ark came to rest in an area known as the mountains of Ararat. This area today is in the country of Turkey. All of Noah’s family and all the animals came out of the ark safely. This means that two of every kind of dinosaur also came out of the Ark, ready to reproduce and spread out across the Earth. Dinosaurs, therefore, must have lived on the Earth after the Flood. People and dinosaurs thus have been living together sometime during the past 4,500 years. Do we have any evidence of this? Actually, yes, we do. Many cultures have mythological creatures such as dragons, that appear very similar to dinosaurs. These depictions may have been exaggerated over the years. There is also an example of an encounter with a dinosaur someone described in England in 1405:

Close to the town of Bures, near Sudbury, there has lately appeared, to the great hurt of the countryside, a dragon, vast in body, with a crested head, teeth like a saw, and a tail extending to an enormous length1

Not many existing animals that we know of today match this description more than a dinosaur. This depiction matches a theropod such as Dilophosaurus or Cryolophosaurus. So, it is very possible that humans have been living with dinosaurs for much longer than previously thought.

The Blue Tongue Skink

by Ebenezer Jiang

Above: A wild Blue Tongue Skink spotted attempting to surround an IOCS member’s home. By Charli Sparke.

The Blue Tongue Skink (Tiliqua)1 is a kind of monitor lizard that lives in Oceania. Created along with all reptiles on the six day of the creation week, Blue-Tongue Skinks are famous for their long, blue tongue. These lizards are omnivores, they love feasting on bugs and insects and eat plant parts, such as berries, fruits, and flowers. The Blue-Tongue Skinks has an ability to lose its tail. What is crazy is that their tails will wiggle and move after detaching from the body. This is an intelligently designed defense system, as it distracts predators and gives the skinks an opportunity to escape.

Another thing that Blue-Tongue Skinks can do is an incredibly neat way of sensing movement. Like snakes, these skinks flick their blue tongues in order to detect chemical particles that are in the air. After pulling in the tongue, sensory cells, which are located on top of the mouth, can detect minute changes in the environment. From a leaf falling to the ground to a mouse scurrying in the distance. Blue-Tongue Skinks stick their tongues in and out very often, as much as 300 times every hour (5 times per minute)! This sharp way of sensing the environment is extremely crucial for life, as Blue-Tongue Skinks live in many terrains, including grasslands, deserts, coastal areas, and scrublands. Without this way of sensing movement, many problems pose a threat to these lizards. Evolutionists claim that this lizard evolved roughly 5.33 million years ago in Australia. But the sensory process that the Blue-Tongue Skink has is vital, not to mention that it is very complicated. All of this shows that an Intelligent Designer purposefully designed the sense for this creature.
